Module 1: The Wonder of Water

Why is Water So Important?

Water is life! It covers over 70% of the Earth's surface and is essential for every living thing. Our bodies are made of about 60% water. It helps us digest food, regulates our body temperature, and carries nutrients to our cells.

The Journey of a Water Droplet: The Water Cycle

Water is constantly moving in a cycle of Evaporation, Condensation, and Precipitation. This means the water you drink today might have been in a dinosaur's puddle millions of years ago!

Module 2: Tracking Our Water Footprint

Water Use Inside Our Homes

The biggest water users at home are toilets and showers. A single toilet flush can use 6-10 liters! Long showers and running taps also contribute significantly. This is our "direct" water footprint.

Water Use Outside Our Homes

Water is also used to grow our food and make our clothes. It takes about 1,800 gallons of water to produce a single pair of jeans! This is our "indirect" water footprint.
